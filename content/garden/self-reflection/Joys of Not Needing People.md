**Is Shared Sorrow = Half Sorrow?** 

In the past, people always have been living in communities and groups. There was this sense of collectivism. But, in the current modern age, why is the world at large more individualistic? Why do people don't need each other anymore. There sure is collectivism out there but the count of such collective beliefs is gradually decreasing.

Technology answers most of our questions here. We dedicate plenty of our time and interactions to the current modern technology, eradicating the need of having something else to fill in our empty time.

# Cons of Individualism

The individualism however, originates the sense of loneliness within ourselves. You see humans, by nature, are not meant to be surviving alone. The sense of strong life establishment we feel within groups and people justifies it. This could be one of the major flaw of opting the individualistic belief.

# Beauty of Individualism

But this isn't everything the individualistic belief offers us. Looking at the brighter side, we as a fish get a whole sea for ourselves. Allowing us to understand life the way we encounter it. Allowing us to decide freely from a range full of options. This could be the power of solitude.

> The freedom from people allows us the freedom to spend our every vacant minute doing whatever we want, whenever we want

The people who benefit from solitude are the ones who wholeheartedly appreciate it. From their perspective, the fear of not being able to attain is never there. They have no one to compete. They drive their own self and thus they compete with their very set records. This very way of thinking is extremely powerful. This is because there's no stopping for them. They just keep on progressing with no directional feedbacks.

## Socialism

Not needing friends allows you to set your own boundaries. Instead of you fitting inside a social circle. The right fit of friends actually finds you. They build around you. This doesn't mean you just cut them off, but instead you will have the mental authority to walk away from the wrong fit of friends. You help people who seek your help and when you need the help, you never expect it from anyone. This allows you to build a mental bubble around you that not only saves you from emotional disappointment but also adds to your ability to stand up by yourself. This way, even though there will be people helping you out. Your mental will always be ready to stand up by yourself never relying on the help of others in the first place.

## Self Adaptation

***Belonging requires us to adapt***

People expects us in many regards to perform how they do, adapt how they do and think how they do. We are never free from the gaze of people. Those judging eyes will always be there and there is simply no escape from them. The more people we surround ourselves with, the more judging eyes are there. This also links to the Hawthorne’s effect[^1].

> “A man can be himself only so long as he is alone; and if he does not love solitude, he will not love freedom; for it is only when he is alone that he is really free.”  
> -- **Arthur Schopenhauer,**  [Essays and Aphorisms](https://www.goodreads.com/work/quotes/20739)

# True Sense of Solitude

Not needing people doesn't imply not associating with people at all but it grants us the possibility to embrace solitude when desired. We can associate with people, befriend them and even develop intimacy, but with the freedom to walk away whenever we please, we can achieve the true self-happiness.
