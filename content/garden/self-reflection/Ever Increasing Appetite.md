**[L’appetit vient en mangeant](https://johnmjennings.com/lappetit-vient-en-mangeant/)** is a french proverb which means "appetite comes from eating"

It means that when you eat something, consume something, you only want more. The more you consume the more you want it.

Lets take eating for an example. There are times when we are not really hungry all but the thought of eating, having something to eat, that very act itself triggers us to eat and creates that sense of hunger inside of us, which maybe wasn't at all in that moment.

### How the Human Hunger System Work

> There are three phases of digestion, the first of which is the Cephalic Phase Response. The other two are the Gastric and Intestinal phases of digestion.

> The Cephalic Phase is triggered by the**sight, smell, taste or even thought of food.**   During the cephalic phase gastric secretions begin,  which include increasing salivation and the secretion of gastric acid, hormones, and insulin. The cephalic phase **signals your body that food is coming** and prepares your body to digest it. Some studies have found that triggering the cephalic phase can create appetite/hunger.

### Relationship with Other Aspects of Life

This thing not only applies to food at all. It applies to just about everything we consume. The information we intake to the interests and habits we develop.

We would only want more the more we consume something.
